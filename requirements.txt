Requirements Document – Unified Security Operations Platform (SIEM + SOAR + XDR + Threat Intelligence + AI Copilot)
Date: May 26, 2026Purpose:Define business, technical, security, and operational requirements for a next-generation Unified Security Operations Platform integrating SIEM, SOAR, XDR, Threat Intelligence, and AI-assisted security operations.
1. Executive Summary
The organization seeks to modernize its Security Operations Center (SOC) by implementing a unified platform that combines SIEM, SOAR, XDR, Threat Intelligence, and AI Copilot capabilities. The goal is to improve detection, investigation, response, compliance, scalability, and operational efficiency while reducing cost and analyst workload.
2. Strategic Objectives
• Reduce alert fatigue and analyst workload• Improve MTTR and incident response• Support global and regional regulatory requirements• Enable AI-assisted investigation and automation• Reduce vendor/tool sprawl• Support hybrid and multi-cloud environments• Provide scalable and cost-efficient telemetry ingestion and retention
3. Scope
In Scope:• SIEM and centralized telemetry• SOAR and playbook orchestration• XDR capabilities• Threat intelligence integration• AI-assisted SOC investigation• Cloud and on-premises monitoring• Compliance and reportingOut of Scope:• Endpoint replacement projects• Network hardware refresh
4. Functional Requirements
SIEM:• Log ingestion and normalization• Correlation and detection rules• Long-term retention and search• Multi-tenant and regional segregation supportSOAR:• Automated response workflows• Ticketing and case management integration• Human approval workflowsXDR:• Endpoint, cloud, identity, and network telemetry• Cross-domain correlationThreat Intelligence:• STIX/TAXII integration• IOC ingestion and enrichment• MITRE ATT&CK mappingAI Copilot:• Natural language investigation• AI-generated incident summaries• Threat reasoning and recommendations• Analyst assist and guided response
5. Technical Requirements
• Cloud-native and hybrid deployment• Security data lake architecture support• OpenTelemetry support where applicable• API-first integration• Detection-as-Code support (Sigma/YARA-L)• Scalability beyond 12TB/day equivalent workloads• High availability and disaster recovery
6. Security and Compliance Requirements
• RBAC and least privilege• Encryption at rest and in transit• Audit logging• Regional data residency controls• Compliance alignment with DORA, GDPR, ECB, and local regulations• Evidence and reporting retention controls
7. AI and Automation Requirements
• AI-assisted alert triage• Autonomous enrichment• AI confidence scoring• Guardrails and analyst approval• Explainable AI outputs
8. Non-Functional Requirements
• 99.9% availability• Low latency search and analytics• Performance monitoring• Vendor support and roadmap maturity
9. Evaluation Criteria
• Functionality• Scalability• AI maturity• Cost and licensing• Regulatory support• Integration ecosystem• Vendor viability
10. Candidate Platforms for Evaluation
• Splunk + Cisco• Microsoft Sentinel + Defender• Google Chronicle• Palo Alto Cortex XSIAM• CrowdStrike Falcon Next-Gen SIEM• IBM QRadar Suite
11. Future-State Vision
The future SOC should evolve toward an AI-assisted and increasingly autonomous operating model, leveraging a unified data and analytics layer while preserving human oversight for critical decisions and governance.
Appendix A – Example Target Architecture
Telemetry Sources → OpenTelemetry / Collectors → Security Data Lake → Detection + AI Analytics → SOAR / XDR Response → Analyst Oversight
